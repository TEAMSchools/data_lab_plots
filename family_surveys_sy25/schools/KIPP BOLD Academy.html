About the Questions

Each question uses a 4-point scale from most student-centered (1) to least (4). Option 5 (“no context”) is excluded from both numerator and denominator. Answers 1 and 2 (highlighted green) count toward the positive agreement rate.

01Student expectations
What best describes expectations for students at your school?
1Students are welcomed to be themselves, bring their full personalities AND family culture, and engage with staff about academic and non-academic topics.
2Students are welcomed to be themselves and bring their full personality to school.
3Students are expected to focus mostly on being respectful, follow directions immediately, and rarely question teachers.
4Students are rarely ever allowed to talk or move without staff's permission or presence.
5I do not have enough context to answer this question.
02Staff-student interactions
What best describes the interactions between staff and students?
1Staff care deeply about every student; staff share their personality and take time to relate to and learn about each student.
2Staff always listen to and treat students with kindness.
3Staff don't really listen to students and talk to them without much joy or excitement.
4Staff frequently call students out publicly, use an angry tone with, or even yell at students.
5I do not have enough context to answer this question.
03Staff response to behavior
What best describes how staff respond to student behavior?
1Staff rely mostly on relationships to address student behavior, rarely ever send a student out of class, and take time to make all students feel heard and valued.
2Staff work to understand students before issuing consequences – when consequences are given, they are fair.
3Staff use the same consequences to address ALL levels of behavior without first working to understand the students or what happened.
4Staff often assign detentions and/or remove students from activities/classroom for even minor issues – OR staff ignore student behavior completely.
5I do not have enough context to answer this question.

07Learning outcomes
What best describes learning outcomes for students at your school?
1Students' performance includes grades earned on tests/quizzes, as well as academic and social goals. Teachers and students work together to set, communicate, and celebrate all goals.
2Students' performance is measured through both what grades they are getting and how they are growing. Teachers often meet with students to discuss growth goals and to track progress.
3Students' performance is measured both by the grades they are getting on tests/quizzes; as well as how much growth they are showing.
4Students' performance is only measured by the grades they are getting on tests/quizzes.

08Instructional practices
What best describes teachers' instructional practices at your school?
1Teachers are committed to challenging and helping all students learn. Teachers create an environment where all students are included and can thrive.
2Teachers create an environment where most students actively participate, and take academic risks to strengthen their own learning. Teachers find many different and creative ways to make learning engaging for most students.
3Teachers plan lessons and use class time similarly each day. Teachers are clear on which students' work meets the expectations, and which students' work do not.
4Teachers generally do not provide enough individualized support to students. Students who don't get the right answer are often left unsure about how to fix their work.

09Lessons & resources
What best describes the lessons and learning resources at your school?
1The lessons, books, and learning resources used by students support the development of who they are as people. Students routinely engage in exploring their own and other cultures, and understand the purpose of what they are learning.
2The lessons, books, and learning resources used at my school have a diverse representation of cultures and perspectives. Students are able to produce work that reflects and connects their own ideas, thoughts, and opinions.
3Some of the lessons, books, and learning resources used at my school show different cultures through photographs and stories. Many students are able to relate to what they are learning about.
4The lessons, books, and learning resources used at my school that don't value diversity and inclusion. Students often feel like they cannot relate to what they are learning about.
5I do not have enough context to answer this question.
10Family engagement
What best describes family engagement at your school?
1Families and teachers at my school communicate frequently and openly about progress, concerns, and updates. Families are active members of the school community.
2Families and teachers at my school communicate often about progress, concerns, and updates. Many families know how to be and are actively involved with student and school-wide events.
3Families and teachers at my school communicate when they need to. Families ask questions and/or share concerns, and generally feel supported when they do.
4Teachers do not often communicate with families at my school. Families are not sure who to go to when they want to learn more, have a concern, or need help.
5I do not have enough context to answer this question.
